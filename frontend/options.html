Digital Carbon Footprint: Awareness & Reduction

Reducing Your Digital Carbon Emissions

Many people don't think about the way that not only their offline but their online activities can contribute to their carbon footprint!! The tips below can help reduce your digital carbon emissions:

  • Stream content in standard definition (e.g. 480p) instead of HD (1080p) or 4K to save up to 75% of energy.
  • Choose browsers like Opera or Firefox that are optimized for lower energy consumption.
  • Prioritize the download of music or podcasts to your device once and replay from local storage instead of streaming multiple times.
  • Regularly delete old emails, especially those with large attachments, to minimize energy used for data storage.
  • Use smart plugs that cut off power supply to fully charged devices or invest in chargers with automatic power-off functionality.
  • Reduce the digital carbon footprint of your website by compressing images using tools like TinyPNG and minifying JavaScript and CSS files.
    • Prioritize your business with businesses that:
  • host their services with eco-friendly web hosting services that utilize renewable energy or those that actively purchase carbon offsets for their web servers.
  • Encourage sustainable web design with fast-loading pages that require less data to view and less energy to host.

These steps have been shown to significantly reduce carbon footprint. Try some out today and help us work towards a more sustainable future!

About Chrome Carbon Tracker

Emissions Analyzer provides an informed estimate of a user's web session's carbon emission usage. We use the standard 0.81 kWh/GB energy-to-data ratio updated via Sustainable Web Design to calculate electricity usage and we also utilize the electricityMaps API for real-time gCO2equiv/kWh data for the specific country that the user is located in.
